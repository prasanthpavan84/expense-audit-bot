[1:00 — ARCHITECTURE]
(show assets/architecture_diagram.png)
Let us walk through the architecture.
First, user inputs go through our security_checkpoint node which scrubs PII and checks for prompt injections.
Next, clean inputs hit the intent_router, directing policy queries to policy_handler, math to calculator, and expense submissions to the audit_orchestrator_node.
The audit_orchestrator_node delegates to receipt_extractor for structured data extraction and policy_verifier for compliance checking.
Both agents use the local Model Context Protocol tools to query company records and policies.
Finally, route_decision routes the transaction: compliant low-value claims flow to finalize_expense for automatic approval, while high-value claims or low-confidence extractions escalate to the human_review node for human approval before finalization.

[2:00 — LIVE DEMO]
(switch to playground at localhost:18081)
Now let us run a live demonstration of three different scenarios.
First, let's paste a policy-compliant expense.
(pause)
Input: "Please audit this expense: Lunch with a client at Pizza Hut on 2026-06-25. Total amount: $35.50 USD. Merchant: Pizza Hut. Items: 2 Pizzas, 1 Salad, 2 Sodas."
(pause)
Notice that the receipt_extractor pulls the structured details, and policy_verifier approves it automatically since the Meals limit is fifty dollars. The final decision is Auto-Approved.

Next, let's submit a high-value claim.
(pause)
Input: "Please audit this expense: Hotel stay for 3 nights during tech conference. Merchant: Hilton Hotels. Date: 2026-06-28. Total amount: $280.00 USD. Items: Room charge."
(pause)
Because this expense is two hundred and eighty dollars, which exceeds the two hundred dollar threshold, the workflow triggers a pause.
(pause)
We see a prompt asking for human auditor input. I will type "approve". The system resumes, finalizing the expense as Approved by Auditor.

Finally, let's submit a restricted purchase.
(pause)
Input: "Please audit this expense: Team celebratory drinks at Gold Club Bar on 2026-06-27. Total amount: $90.00 USD. Items: Beer, cocktails."
(pause)
The merchant name contains the word "Bar", which is a restricted category. The verifier detects this policy violation and automatically rejects the submission. The final decision is Denied.
